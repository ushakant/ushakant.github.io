Chapter 1: Working in A&E

I was working in A&E in Birmingham hospital as a Junior Doctor, when Covid hit. There were some very difficult situations where streams of people were coming in with a respiratory illness that no-one at the time knew much about. Nothing had prepared me for what was to come.

Chapter 2: Working as a Junior Doctor

In my lunchtime at Birmingham univsersity, ITV news wanted to hear first hand what it was like to work in A&E and my experiences of the pressures of working as a Doctor during Covid.

Chapter 3: Steths to Decks

So, how did this come about, swapping my stethescope for decks, you might ask? From day to night. I thought to myself, what can I do to boost the morale of my colleagues in the NHS who were relentlessly working under very difficult conditions - some not even seeing their families. With my passion for music I decided to do some DJ sessions from my kitchen to lift our spirits. It very quickly spiralled and went viral and before I knew it I was being tuned in by healthcare professionals nationally, news broadcasters hunted me down for interviews ITV, BBC, Sky News to name just a few.

TomorrowLand Festival

I performed as a DJ in the prestigious festival TommorowLand in Belgium, which fueled my passion further to combine my passion to forge a career of working as a Doctor and a DJ.
